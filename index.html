Practice Buddy
Helping you practice efficiently

This music practice timer is inspired by the work of Molly Gebrian and incorporates some of her suggestions for efficient practice.

"Micro-breaks" are 10–20 second pauses that allow your brain to consolidate what you were just practicing.

The app encourages working in chunks of 5–10 minutes, with 1–3 minute rests between chunks.

Interleaved practice is encouraged — choose a different piece, passage, etude, or technique for each chunk. See Molly's work for the compelling research behind this.

Recording yourself is encouraged! Each practice round is recorded. Review it using the "waveform" button in the micro-break. You can turn recordings off in Settings.

Voice control allows you to run your entire practice session without touching your phone.

If voice control is not what you want, the keyboard shortcuts work with a "page turner" foot pedal (that can emulate a "SPACE" or "ENTER" key.) They eliminate most of your need to touch the screen during a normal practice flow.

If "Automatically advance" is on in Settings, it automatically advances to the micro-break at the end of a practice round. If it is off, you get an audible notification when a practice round ends, but you must skip to the next phase manually.

To add this app to your iPhone or iPad home screen, tap the Share button in Safari, then tap "Add to Home Screen."

If the record/review feature is not working on iPhone or iPad, go to Settings → Safari → Microphone and make sure it is not set to "Deny."

The microphone indicator stays on between practice rounds — this is intentional, to avoid re-requesting microphone permission. Recording only occurs during the Practice phase.

Practice Buddy
Helping you practice efficiently
Practice Buddy:
  • Asks you "intentional practice" questions and offers reminders that you can customize.
  • Automatically records each "round" of practice.
  • Reminds you to take "micro-breaks" between rounds, where you can review your recording with one click.
  • Has a "rep counter" that counts "correct" repetitions hands-free.
    • Say "good" for success or "wrong" for a failure.
    • Add more-expressive synonyms for "good" and "wrong" in settings .

Tap (lower right) for more info on efficient practice.

Voice control lets you operate the app hands-free, but it does drain the battery faster.

Voice commands include "start", "play", "pause", "done", and "ready".

Say "reps" to open/close the rep-counter and "good" or "wrong" after a good/bad rep (see settings to customize).

Say "review" to review your recording during micro-breaks... "re-play" to re-play it and "close" to exit the review.

In a car (or wherever connected to Bluetooth audio) with voice control, sound will come through your phone. Without, it will come through the Bluetooth speakers. This is a limitation of web apps.

Crash-recovery testing

Simulate crash + reload tells the watchdog to treat this session as crashed (skip the clean-shutdown marker on pagehide). Press 3 times in a row to trigger automatic recovery.

Reset boot counter zeros the bad-boot count without recovery — useful if a real crash counted falsely.

Reset app unregisters the service worker and deletes all caches, then reloads. Use this if the app is stuck or behaving strangely.
